# Customer Churn Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most important challenges faced by telecom companies. Understanding why customers leave can help businesses improve customer retention and reduce revenue loss.

In this project, I analyzed the Telco Customer Churn dataset using PostgreSQL to identify the key factors influencing customer churn and uncover actionable business insights.

## Project Objective

The analysis focuses on customer contracts, internet services, support services, billing methods, monthly charges, customer tenure, and revenue impact.

## Dataset Information

- Dataset: Telco Customer Churn
- Rows: 7,043
- Columns: 21
- Industry: Telecommunications
- Target Variable: Churn

## SQL Business Questions

1. What is the overall churn rate?

2. Which contract type has the highest churn?

3. Does Fiber Optic service have a higher churn rate than other internet services?

4. Do customers with Tech Support churn less?

5. Do customers with Online Security churn less?

6. Do customers with higher monthly charges churn more?

7. Which payment method has the highest churn?

8. Does paperless billing influence churn?

9. Are senior citizens more likely to churn?

10. Do customers with partners churn less?

11. Do customers with dependents churn less?

12. How much revenue is lost due to churned customers?

13. What is the average tenure of churned vs retained customers?

14. What are the characteristics of high-risk churn customers?

## Key Findings

* The overall customer churn rate was **26.58%**.

* Customers with **month-to-month contracts** had the highest churn rate.

* Customers using **Fiber Optic internet service** were more likely to leave compared to customers using other internet services.

* Customers who did not have **Tech Support** or **Online Security** services showed higher churn rates.

* Customers with **higher monthly charges** were more likely to churn.

* Among all payment methods, customers using **Electronic Check** had the highest churn rate.

* **Senior citizens** were more likely to churn than non-senior customers.

* Customers without **partners** or **dependents** had higher churn rates.

* The company lost around **$2.86 million** in revenue from customers who churned.

* Customers who churned had a **shorter average tenure** compared to customers who stayed.

* The customers most likely to churn were those with **month-to-month contracts**, **Fiber Optic internet service**, and no **Tech Support** or **Online Security** services.

## Recommendations

* Encourage customers to switch from month-to-month contracts to long-term plans through discounts and loyalty programs.

* Promote Tech Support and Online Security services to improve customer retention.

* Investigate the reasons behind the high churn rate among Fiber Optic customers.

* Develop retention strategies targeting Electronic Check users and senior citizens.

* Focus on retaining new customers during their early months with the company.

* Monitor high-risk customer segments and take proactive measures before they churn.
